FAQ

Common questions about regulatory fraud data sharing

What fraud data sharing platforms do you support?

We currently support the following platforms:

FNC-RF (Fichier National des Comptes à Risques et des Fraudes) — The Banque de France's national file for at-risk accounts and fraud. We help French regulated banks share fraud typology data, mule account indicators, and suspicious account information.

EU FRIDA (Fraud Reporting and Information-sharing for the Detection and Assessment of fraud) — The ECB's pan-European fraud data sharing initiative. We help banks participate by structuring and exchanging fraud indicators across EU member institutions.

We also support MISP-based systems, STIX/TAXII-based exchanges, and custom API integrations. If your regulator has a specific platform requirement, we can adapt to it.

My regulator's platform isn't listed. Can you support my regulator?
Yes. We've worked with regulators globally and can support any structured data exchange requirement. We'll work with you to understand your regulator's specific format, reporting cadence, and connectivity requirements, then configure the platform accordingly.
Can you help me start a fraud data sharing community?
Absolutely. We have extensive experience designing and launching fraud sharing communities — including work on Australia's national threat sharing program. We help with governance design, infrastructure, member onboarding, and ongoing community operations. Learn more about starting a sharing community.
Can you also help me share cyber threat intelligence?
Yes. Many of our banking customers share both fraud data and cyber threat intelligence. We provide the same managed platform, integration, and workflow services for CTI sharing — including MISP-based, STIX/TAXII, and custom formats. Learn more about our cyber threat intelligence services.
