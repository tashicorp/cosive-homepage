Consume & share fraud data

Meet regulatory fraud reporting requirements by sharing structured fraud intelligence directly with your banking regulator through MISP-based data exchange.

The compliance challenge

Regulators worldwide are mandating structured fraud data sharing — each with its own formats, reporting cadences, and compliance expectations. Without the right tooling, this means manual data wrangling and constant risk of non-compliance.

How we help

  • Structured fraud data exchange — Regulator-ready formats using MISP taxonomies and sharing groups built for each framework.
  • Automated reporting workflows — Scheduled, policy-driven exports matching each regulator's requirements.
  • Multi-framework support — One MISP instance satisfying multiple frameworks simultaneously.
  • Expert guidance — We've worked directly with regulators to shape these frameworks and help you navigate compliance.

Consume and share fraud data with other banks

Exchange fraud intelligence directly with peer institutions. Build trusted bilateral and multilateral sharing relationships to detect fraud faster and reduce losses across the banking sector.

The challenge

Banks face fraud from the same criminal networks, but often detect it in isolation. Without shared intelligence, one bank's blocked fraudster simply moves to the next. Manual sharing via spreadsheets and email is slow, error-prone, and doesn't scale.

How we help

  • Peer-to-peer sharing channels — Establish direct, encrypted fraud data exchange with partner banks using MISP sharing groups.
  • Mule account intelligence — Share and receive mule account indicators in near real-time to block fraudulent transactions before settlement.
  • Fraud typology exchange — Collectively identify emerging fraud patterns by sharing structured typology data across institutions.
  • Trust framework management — Control exactly what you share with whom using MISP's granular distribution levels and sharing group policies.

Start your own fraud data sharing community

Launch and operate a trusted fraud intelligence sharing community for your region, sector, or industry group using Cosive's proven community-building methodology and CloudMISP infrastructure.

Why start a community

Individual banks sharing bilaterally only see part of the picture. A structured community multiplies the intelligence available to every member — smaller banks gain visibility they could never achieve alone, and larger banks benefit from broader coverage of regional fraud patterns.

What we provide

  • Community governance design — Membership criteria, data sharing agreements, and operating procedures tailored to your sector.
  • CloudMISP infrastructure — A dedicated, managed MISP instance with sharing groups, taxonomies, and access controls pre-configured for your community.
  • Onboarding support — Hands-on help bringing member institutions onto the platform with training and integration assistance.
  • Ongoing operations — Threat intelligence curation, community facilitation, and platform management so the community thrives.

Integrate fraud data into your security tools

Connect your fraud management platforms, case management systems, and transaction monitoring tools so shared intelligence flows automatically where it's needed most.

The integration challenge

Fraud teams juggle multiple tools that don't talk to each other — transaction monitoring, case management, blocklists, and threat intelligence platforms all operate in silos. Manually copying indicators between systems wastes time and introduces gaps that fraudsters exploit.

How we help

  • Pre-built connectors — Ready-made integrations for SAS, NICE Actimize, Featurespace, Splunk, and other major fraud platforms.
  • Automated indicator flow — Fraud indicators from shared intelligence feed directly into detection rules and blocklists without manual intervention.
  • Flexible API layer — MISP's REST API and PyMISP library let you build custom integrations for any internal system.
  • Bidirectional sync — Not just one-way feeds — push local detections back to the sharing community to benefit all members.
